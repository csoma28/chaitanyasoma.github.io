Kaleidoscope: Engineering Real Variety in LLM Ideation

Honda Research Institute USA

When research teams use large language models for brainstorming, the systems repeatedly produce the same three ideas dressed in different words. Kaleidoscope is a system that measures, explains, and corrects this "false variety" so AI-assisted ideation actually expands the possibility space.

The Problem

Three ideas in different costumes

Honda Research Institute's innovation teams kept hitting the same wall when prompting LLMs to brainstorm new mobility concepts. "AI assistant for drivers," "Smart co-pilot for cars," "Vehicle intelligence companion" — identical ideas, different vocabulary. The output looked diverse linguistically but represented one concept.

This creates a compounding cost: research time wasted evaluating duplicates, portfolios that look varied but aren't, and no shared definition of what "new" actually means.

Our Approach

System designers, not model engineers

Rather than train new models, we built a measurement and steering layer that sits on top of existing LLMs. The system has three connected components:

  • Idea representation. Encode generated ideas as embedding vectors plus structured attributes (user, context, problem type, technology lever) so they become comparable objects rather than free text.
  • Similarity & novelty scoring. Cosine-similarity thresholds calibrated against human judgment, paired with cluster-distance metrics, to distinguish duplicates from genuinely distinct ideas.
  • Controlled generation. Prompt strategies, dimension forcing, and rejection sampling that push the model into under-explored regions of the idea space.
Workflow Options

Three deliverable shapes, one core engine

A
Human Ideation Handbook. Engineers run a structured dimension framework; the tool validates novelty.
B
Human + AI Workflow. Humans direct the AI through targeted prompts; the system scores and explains overlaps.
C
Agentic AI Loop. An autonomous agent generates, embeds, scores, rejects, and iterates until the idea library is diverse.

The recommendation: deliver Option A as the methodology backbone and Option B as the automation layer on top — locking the framework first, then wiring the MVP demo.

Building Blocks

Nine concrete engineering tasks

  • Idea representation schema — semantic vectors plus structured fields defining what an "idea" is.
  • Similarity score with calibrated thresholds (>0.85 duplicate, 0.65–0.85 similar, <0.45 distinct).
  • Novelty score measuring distance to the nearest existing idea or cluster center.
  • Idea clusters via k-means / hierarchical / density-based methods to label concept families.
  • Repetition filter rule driving the agentic loop and the human review step.
  • Diversity objective — pairwise similarity, cluster coverage, entropy, novelty rate.
  • Explainability logic that surfaces why two ideas overlap in plain language.
  • Exploration strategy for steering the system into under-populated regions.
  • Evaluation framework proving the system actually improves ideation against baselines.
Why It Matters

Turning ideation into a measurable capability

For Honda Research Institute, the goal is to break out of repetitive ideation patterns and access genuinely diverse innovation directions across Honda's four research domains. The deliverable isn't a model — it's a working prototype, an open-source codebase, and a transferable framework that any team can apply to assess, refine, and expand AI-generated ideas.

"Final acceptance is based on clarity, usefulness, and explainability, not model accuracy alone."
