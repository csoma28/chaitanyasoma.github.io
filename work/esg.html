ESG Materiality Assessment for an African Aquaculture Leader

FirstWave Group / Yalelo Zambia • 180 Degrees Consulting Bath

A six-month consulting engagement to design a stakeholder-anchored ESG materiality framework for one of Sub-Saharan Africa's largest aquaculture operations. The deliverable: a defensible, repeatable methodology that the client could carry into their next sustainability reporting cycle.

Context

What does sustainability actually mean for a fish farm in Zambia?

FirstWave Group runs one of the largest tilapia operations in Africa through its Yalelo brand. To strengthen its ESG positioning with investors, regulators, and local stakeholders, the company needed a structured way to identify which sustainability issues actually mattered to its business and to the communities it operates in.

The challenge: materiality is often handled as a checkbox exercise. We needed a framework grounded in real stakeholder priorities, not generic ESG templates — one tailored to aquaculture and the Zambian operating context.

My Role

Engagement Manager for a 7-person team

I led project scoping, client communication, and the workstream split across three pillars: stakeholder analysis, metric identification, and survey construction. Day to day this meant running internal team syncs, briefing the client on progress, sequencing deliverables, and quality-checking the analytical outputs before they reached the client.

Approach

Three workstreams, one integrated output

  • Stakeholder analysis. Combined desk research with internal interviews across Yalelo's commercial, production, and security teams to identify 15 distinct external stakeholder groups, from government regulators to local healthcare providers, peers, and traditional leadership.
  • Prioritisation. Built a Power-Interest matrix and a Power-Urgency-Mandate analysis, scoring each group on qualitative interview signals and quantitative survey responses. Local Government, Government Regulators, and Healthcare emerged as the top-priority stakeholders.
  • Metric identification. Pulled from global standards (SASB, IFC, GRI, ISO) and aquaculture-specific frameworks (ASC, FAO), then refined through MECE filtering to land on 34 ESG metrics spanning Environmental, Social, and Governance pillars.
  • Survey construction. Designed a Microsoft Forms instrument with three sub-questions per metric (internal importance, external importance, relevant stakeholder) so the company could continue running the assessment after our engagement ended.

Why It Mattered

From one-time deliverable to repeatable process

The strongest part of the engagement wasn't the final ranking — it was the methodology. By formalising the dimensions, scoring criteria, and survey instruments, we left FirstWave with a process they could re-run as their stakeholder landscape shifted. The work fed directly into their next reporting cycle and the engagement strategies for their highest-priority stakeholder groups.

"If this stakeholder prioritisation analysis is scaled up, standard deviation should be used to better understand the range of possible scores within a stakeholder group." — A note we left for the next analyst, baked into the handover documentation.
