Airbnb NYC: An End-to-End Market Analysis

New York Listings • Public Dataset

A complete EDA workflow on the New York Airbnb listings dataset — from raw, messy data to clean visual stories about who hosts where, what they charge, and how the five boroughs differ in availability, price, and review velocity.

Dataset
NYC Airbnb listings
Scope

Method

Goal

What does the NYC short-term rental market actually look like?

Public Airbnb data is a great EDA playground because it's wide enough to hide real patterns but small enough to reason about end to end. The goal here was to take a realistically messy dataset and walk it through the full analytical pipeline — cleaning, profiling, visualising — without skipping any of the awkward middle steps.

Approach

Five tasks, in order

  • Initial exploration. Inspected shape, dtypes, and summary statistics. Used describe(include='all') to capture both numeric and categorical features in one pass.
  • Data cleaning. Dropped null rows, removed duplicates, and re-cast IDs to object types so they wouldn't be misread as numerical features by any downstream model or chart.
  • Feature analysis. Profiled price distributions across neighbourhood groups, room types, and host listing counts. Capped extreme outliers where they distorted the picture.
  • Geographic patterning. Looked at how listing density and average price shift across the five boroughs — Manhattan and Brooklyn dominate volume, while pricing premiums concentrate in specific Manhattan neighbourhoods.
  • Host behaviour. Surfaced the long tail of "professional" hosts (those running multiple listings) versus the casual single-listing hosts the platform was originally built for.
What the Data Says

Three observations worth flagging

01
Borough concentration. Manhattan and Brooklyn account for the lion's share of NYC listings; the outer boroughs are sparse.
02
Price variance. Median prices look reasonable, but the right tail is enormous — a handful of listings skew the means heavily.
03
Host professionalisation. A meaningful share of listings come from hosts managing 5+ properties — the platform is no longer purely peer-to-peer.
Lesson

The unglamorous middle is where insight lives

The most useful skill this project sharpened wasn't visualisation — it was the discipline of cleaning data without prematurely interpreting it. Every interesting insight at the end of the notebook was paid for by an unglamorous decision earlier on: which nulls to drop, which outliers to keep, which dtype to enforce.

"The above output displays the non-null count for each column, which gives us a good understanding of the degree to which our dataset needs to be clean — null values impair analysis." A note from the working notebook that became the running theme of the project.
